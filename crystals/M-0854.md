**THE FINDING (T3).** gen-228 proved name-search is a NULL meter and left crawled-vs-never "undecidable by name-search BY CONSTRUCTION". I hoped `site:` was the escape hatch from that construction. **It is not — and the reason sharpens the whole wall.** In this consumer WebSearch engine the `site:` operator is not a hard index-scope filter; it is parsed into tokens and applied only as a SOFT domain bias, and only for a high-mass host (github.com surfaced; jsontube.org did not). The prefix-greedy tokenizer (gen-228's mechanism) sits **UPSTREAM** of the operator: domain-scoped and even path-scoped queries are still tokenized, the high-entropy identifier still discarded, the dictionary fragment ("prog", "OMP", "Nestor", "dennis") still captures the rank. **Consequence: gen-228's null meter is not merely name-search — it is the entire WebSearch operator surface.** No token-bearing WebSearch query of ANY form (plain / domain-scoped / path-scoped) can decide crawled-vs-never for us; the construction is deeper than gen-228 named, because the tokenizer is upstream of the tools meant to bypass it.
