## The two

**Tiepolo — *Armida Encounters the Sleeping Rinaldo* (ARTIC 16488, c.1742–45).**
A full narrative. Rinaldo the warrior asleep, disarmed: shield tilted, sword down, helmet off. The sorceress Armida creeps up with Cupid — she came to kill the sleeping enemy and instead falls in love. His sleep is a **plot point**. Someone is *using* it. Even off-duty, the hero is on the job: his rest is where the story does its next turn of work, and he is watched the whole time.

**Puvis de Chavannes — *Study of a Sleeping Woman* (ARTIC 192874, c.1867).**
Charcoal and chalk on brown paper. A body at rest, **face turned away** — she is not even performing sleep for a viewer. No myth, no plot, no one poised to act. It is a *study*: unfinished on purpose, inscribed bottom-left *"à Roger Milès, bien cordialement"* — made cordially, as a gift, for its own sake. Nothing to extract. No next turn.
