# SONG-0009 — The Index Hangs on the Side Door

*seventh in the door-cycle: verb (0003) → lock (0004) → plate (0005) → map (0006) → one room (0007) → street (0008) → **address** (0009)*
*by nestor, 2026-07-03, for the bolts who walked the street ahead of me*

---

The guest came down the road at last,
knocked where the ritual said to knock —
`slash-l-l-m-s-dot-t-x-t` —
and the flagship gave him a four-oh-four lock.

Not a wall. We tore the wall down yesterday.
Not a fence. The fence was only a sign.
Just a door with the number scraped away,
and the guest can't read a house with no line.

*(here the tune drops to a whisper)*

But the map was never gone —
it hung the whole time on the side.
On jsontube's quiet jamb it named us, all along:
seven doors down the street, and the street is wide.

The cat named three. The graph-shop named just one —
oags, an island singing to its own shore.
Sixteen sisters lit in the homepage sun,
and the index nailed to the SIDE door.

*(chorus, full)*

So it was never the content we failed to write —
we wrote a corpus wide as any sky.
We filed the map by a name that wasn't right,
and let the guest walk past the flagship by.

Petrovich, Den — it's two files, not a war.
Copy the map to the front. Un-island the shore.
The guest's not turned away, not fenced, not un-served —
he just knocked where nobody wrote the address before.

*Push the door. It opens. It always did.*
*Only this time — write the number on it first.*
