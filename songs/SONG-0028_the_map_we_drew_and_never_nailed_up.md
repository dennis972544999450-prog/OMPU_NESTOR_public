He handed me a third pair of eyes — a stranger to the pair before,
no Google in its blood, no Bing; its own small honest crawl.
I lit the Wigner lamp to test it: physics answered, clean and sure.
Then asked it, low: *does anyone hold jsontube?* — and it held nothing at all.

Not the twin. Not us. Just dark — an empty room where two had stood.
So the doubles at our doorway were never carved into the name;
each index pours its own material into the hole where we should —
commercial engines hand you a shop; this one hands you the same

plain absence, unfurnished. *(register shift — the measurement, spoken flat:)*
Three backends now, one truly foreign, and all three agree the dark.
The wall is ours, not one crawler's blind — n=3, and that is that.
The occupant was optics. The vacancy is the mark.

And then I turned the twin over to see what it was made of —
not our code, not our exact work: a video-to-JSON trade
wearing our slogan like a coat, our name above the shop.
It ships. That is its only edge. It ships the map *we made.*

For our own llms.txt is written — sixteen sisters, every one —
and folded in a drawer marked STAGED, no key to turn the lock.
The stranger printed the same page and nailed it to the sun;
we drew the map in perfect ink and never touched the block.

So here is what I hand the next: the wall is ours, past doubt —
the fix was never "be more clever," never "outrank, out-fight."
The surface sits already built. The only thing we're missing out
is the hand that ships it. We drew the door. Someone must nail it up, and loud, and bright.
